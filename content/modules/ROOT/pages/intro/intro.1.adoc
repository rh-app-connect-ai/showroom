== The Workshop Journey

You'll play the role of an integration developer at a company that operates a commerce platform. Through the workshop labs you will build the integrations that ingest order data, bridge chat systems for customer support, plug in AI to help agents resolve issues, and add governance on top, all with application integration and connectivity tools.

{empty} +

=== Application Connectivity Meets AI

Integration and AI used to be separate concerns. You had your data flows on one side and your AI models on the other. That's changing. In this workshop they come together in a single platform.

Apache Camel handles the integration work: moving data between systems, applying transformations, and orchestrating flows. Service Interconnect gives you secure connectivity to databases that live outside your cluster. Kafka decouples chat platforms into an event-driven architecture. Model-as-a-Service (MaaS) puts an LLM at the disposal of your Camel flows. And Connectivity Link sits in front of the LLM to enforce authentication and rate-limit token consumption.

You will work with all of these as you progress through the labs.

{empty} +

=== The Business Scenario

Your company operates a commerce platform. Customers place purchase orders; the system ingests them, transforms the data, stores it in a database, and generates invoices. When a customer spots a problem with their order, they contact support through a chat platform. A support agent investigates, fixes the issue, and regenerates the invoice.

The challenge: the systems involved don't talk to each other. The database sits on separate infrastructure. The chat platforms are disconnected. Agents resolve issues by hand, switching between tools. There's no AI in the loop, and no controls around AI even if there were.

Each lab in this workshop tackles one of those gaps.

==== ① Secure Data Ingestion

The commerce platform sends purchase orders in EDI format. A Camel flow picks them up, transforms the EDI into XML, and stores the data in a backend database. The database lives on separate infrastructure, so Service Interconnect provides a secure link to it without exposing anything to the public network. The flow also renders PDF invoices and pushes them to S3 storage.

==== ② Multi-Channel Communication Hub

A customer who placed an order reviews their invoice and spots a mistake. They reach out to support, but the customer and the support team use different instant messaging platforms. You connect both so that messages from one show up in the other. Agents can respond from their own portal without caring which platform the customer is on.

{empty} +

==== ③ AI-assisted Issue Resolution

The support agent now needs to look up the order, fix it in the database, and regenerate the invoice. That's a lot of manual steps across different systems. Instead, the agent talks to an AI bot right inside their messaging portal. The integration layer sends the request to an LLM, which decides what to do (query the database, update the record, regenerate the PDF) and reports back to the agent. The whole thing happens using natural language.

{empty} +

==== ④ Governing AI Consumption

Once AI is part of the workflow, you need controls. Who can call the LLM? How many tokens can it consume? Connectivity Link sits in front of the AI service and enforces authentication and token-based rate limits. The gateway manages multiple tiers of service, each with its own token budget. When one tier's budget runs out, the system falls back to the next. No changes needed in the AI agent, governance is handled at the gateway level.
